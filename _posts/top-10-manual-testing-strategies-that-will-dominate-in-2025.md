## 2. User-Centric Testing: Focus on Real-World Scenarios

By 2025, **user-centric testing** will dominate manual testing practices, emphasizing real-world user behaviors and usability improvements. With growing user expectations and diversity, testers will employ role-playing techniques, simulate scenarios across wide demographics, and prioritize human-centered feedback.

For example, testing fintech apps for diverse user personas will involve evaluating accessibility needs and financial literacy levels to ensure widespread usability.

6. Optimizing Ultra-Agile Testing Models

With **Agile** methodologies dominating, manual testers in 2025 must adapt to accelerated release cycles and tightly timed sprints. Successful ultra-agile testing will prioritize: 
- Lean objectives anchored to immediate deliverables.
- Collaborative real-time feedback alongside developers.
- Dynamic workflows reflecting the project’s evolution.

By focusing on speed, quality deliberation, and adaptability, manual testers in ultra-agile environments will ensure high standards.

7. Testing Products for Accessibility & Inclusivity

Accessibility and inclusivity discussions will reshape testing by 2025. Manual testing ensures the full potential of universal usability by going beyond automated results.

Activities such as observing compatibility with screen readers, validating navigational accessibility, and ensuring appropriate color contrasts will mitigate barriers for users of all abilities. Prioritizing accessibility will meet legal regulations and align with equality values.

Regression testing often becomes overwhelming due to frequent development updates. In 2025, manual testers will adopt smarter regression strategies powered by AI risk-analysis tools like **Zof AI**. Exploratory testing complements automation workflows, delivering expansive coverage through creativity. Manual testers in 2025 will focus on documenting exploratory results for automation conversion.
