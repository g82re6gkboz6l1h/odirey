# Top 5 Manual Testing Trends to Dominate in 2025

Quality Assurance (QA) remains a crucial part of the software development lifecycle. Though automated solutions are on the rise, manual software testing retains its importance by addressing areas where human intuition and empathy excel. As we approach 2025, significant trends will reshape manual testing to align with evolving user needs, technological advances, and market demands. In this article, we examine five standout manual testing trends that QA professionals and organizations should prepare for.

## 1. Hyper-Personalized Testing Focused on UX

With the rise of hyper-personalization, customer expectations demand tailored software experiences. Hyper-personalized testing will address narrow user segments and specific use cases, helping QA teams devise scenario-driven testing strategies. By stepping into the shoes of diverse customers—such as first-time users versus experienced buyers—manual testers will uncover unique usability issues. Emphasis will shift toward understanding user behaviors, perceptions, and experiences. Collaboration with cross-functional teams to build laser-focused strategies will transform customer-centric manual testing as a core QA practice by 2025.

3. Testing Across Diverse Devices & Ecosystems

As IoT devices, wearables, and next-gen hardware proliferate, the demand for seamless cross-platform functionality will escalate. Manual testers must excel in ensuring compatibility and usability across an increasingly fragmented hardware and software landscape. From testing smartwatch-to-smartphone connections to validating IoT-driven home automation systems, QA teams in 2025 will need to refine strategies for real-time device interactions. The rise of 5G and edge computing will further expand the scope of interconnected system operations, amplifying the requirement for manual hardware testing expertise.

---

4. Sustainability Meets Manual Testing

Sustainability is reshaping the priorities for businesses and software testing alike. By 2025, eco-conscious testing practices will include assessing applications for lower energy consumption, efficient data handling, and ethical governance approaches. Remote testing will continue reducing the environmental footprint associated with physical QA centers. QA testers will also embrace accessibility testing as part of sustainable and inclusive practices, ensuring software solutions are functional for all users, including differently-abled individuals. Platforms like [Zof AI](https://zof.ai) will simplify cloud testing, fortifying sustainability initiatives through scalable, remote-friendly tools.
