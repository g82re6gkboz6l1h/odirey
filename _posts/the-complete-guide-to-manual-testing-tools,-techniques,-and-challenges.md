## Understanding Manual Testing
Manual testing involves testers executing test cases without automation tools, relying on intuition and exploration to discover bugs and improve usability. Here's why it remains crucial:

### Advantages:
- **Adaptability:** Perfect for dynamic software requirements.
- **Intuition:** Captures elements like user satisfaction and accessibility unmet by automation.
- **Creativity-focused:** Empowers exploratory testing to uncover critical glitches.

Despite advantages, manual testing has challenges—time limitations, scalability issues, and human errors. However, combining robust techniques and supportive tools enhances outcomes.

## Techniques of Manual Testing
Harness diverse approaches for a thorough evaluation of applications:

### 1. **Exploratory Testing:** Testers investigate apps without structured test cases, promoting creativity.
### 2. **Ad-Hoc Testing:** Informal testing identifies hidden discrepancies unpredictably.
### 3. **Smoke Testing:** Verifies crucial functionality—the foundation of further evaluations.
### 4. **Regression Testing:** Confirms new updates do not disturb validated features.
### 5. **UI Testing:** Ensures design consistency and navigational ease.
### 6. **Performance Testing:** Includes manual efforts to assess system behavior during stress.

Each technique targets specific challenges, collectively delivering comprehensive software validation.
